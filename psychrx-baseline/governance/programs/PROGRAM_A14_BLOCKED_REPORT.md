## Architectural Risks Avoided

Blocking Program A14 avoids:

- scientific content population without a validated source corpus;
- stimulant medication content without controlled substance governance;
- cardiovascular, abuse, diversion, sleep, appetite and growth metadata without validated field traceability;
- Safety Engine consumption before publication gate;
- Evidence Runtime linkage before source validation;
- premature ADHD medication modeling before earlier medication-class pipelines are proven;
- false impression of clinical readiness;
- accidental creation of treatment recommendation or prescribing logic.

## Prohibited Execution

Until this block is resolved, the following are prohibited:

- populating ADHD medication scientific fields;
- creating stimulant or non-stimulant medication profiles;
- creating ADHD medication safety mappings;
- creating pediatric monitoring content;
- integrating ADHD medications with the Safety Engine;
- integrating ADHD medication content with the Evidence Runtime;
- publishing ADHD medication content into the official knowledge base;
- using ADHD medication content in clinical reasoning;
- generating treatment recommendations;
- generating prescription-oriented outputs.

## Required Conditions to Unblock

Program A14 can be reconsidered only after:

1. Program A03 completes source corpus intake and editorial validation;
2. Programs A04 through A13 are completed in order, or a formal CTO exception ADR is approved;
3. the scientific population workflow has passed at least one complete medication-class validation cycle;
4. controlled-substance metadata governance is defined;
5. pediatric monitoring governance is defined;
6. cardiovascular safety field requirements are validated;
7. abuse and diversion metadata requirements are validated;
8. Evidence Runtime and Safety Engine compatibility gates are confirmed;
9. NEXT_MISSION is updated by governance to authorize Program A14.
